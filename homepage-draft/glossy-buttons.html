Glossy CTA Explorations

Four surface finish directions for Hiku's primary buttons. Each uses a cursor-tracking highlight that follows your mouse — like light catching on polished lacquer. On mobile, the highlight gently drifts on its own.

Desktop: hover & move cursor across buttons  ·  Mobile: tap and drag across buttons

A — Lacquer Drop

Sharp catchlight, rim light, deep body. Clean and confident.

B — Polished Stone

Warm-tinted highlight, subsurface glow. Richer, more organic.

C — Glass Gel

Soft diffused highlight, thin bright edges. Lightest, most subtle.

D — Nail Lacquer

Intense sharp specular, deep convexity. Most faithful to reference.

Color Variants (Nail Lacquer finish)
Accent — Vivid Pink

For secondary CTAs or promotional moments. Pink as accent only.

Light — On Dark

Ghost/outline variant with glossy surface. For dark backgrounds.

Opacity Spectrum — Opaque to Sheer
E1 — Full Opaque

Thick lacquer. Zero translucency. Deepest shadows, heaviest body. Like a solid drop of nail polish.

E2 — Semi-Opaque

Rich and solid, but with a hint of translucent edge glow. Same family as the Nail Lacquer (D) above.

E3 — Semi-Sheer

Color is present but translucent — background peeks through slightly. Like tinted glass or a gel polish first coat.

E4 — Sheer

Glassy color wash. Background clearly visible. Backdrop blur adds frosted dimension. Like sheer lip gloss.

E5 — Ultra Sheer

Barely tinted clear glass. Just enough color to know it's there. Maximum backdrop blur. Dark text for contrast.
