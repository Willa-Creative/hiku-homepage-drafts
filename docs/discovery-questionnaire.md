## Founder Story
Harper's personal experience: after experimenting with online products that didn't suit her skin, she ended up with horrible breakouts, extreme sensitivity, and low self-esteem. The only products her derm could recommend were clinical drugstore brands. She wondered: "Why doesn't a brand exist that feels elevated, but is still gentle enough for my skin?" Collaborated with world-renowned skin experts to design a safe-but-joyful solution. "It's what she wishes she'd had from the start."

**Note:** Harper is 15 years old — audience will likely veer younger with her as founder.

## Website Inspiration Sites
1. **Bop Bop World** — Playfulness done with taste. Easter eggs, interactive moments, joy. Youthful without skewing childish. Delight + elevation coexist.
2. **Summer Fridays** (summerfridays.com) — Shoppable but still a brand flagship. Homepage balances commerce + storytelling. Product carousels and UGC feel curated, not noisy.
3. **In Common With** (incommonwith.com/collections/all-products) — Thoughtful interaction design. PLP toggle for daytime vs. nighttime lighting. Makes online experience feel considered and immersive. Inspiration for communicating skincare textures/finishes/routines digitally.
4. **Aesop** (aesop.com) — Smart merchandising for broad uniform-packaged assortment. Clear hierarchy, thoughtful grouping, guided pathways. Reduces cognitive load. Product comparison modules help users understand nuance through approachable language. Calm, confident, intentional.
5. **Monastery Made** (monasterymade.com) — Good balance between commerce and storytelling. Narrative-driven but shoppable. Content unfolds naturally alongside products. Guides toward routines by concern. (Note: "too grown up in execution for hiku")

## Website Goals
- **A true brand flagship** — primary expression of hiku brand world, not just a store
- Communicate POV, values, and visual language immediately
- Guide through discovery, education, routine-building with confidence
- Offer experiences/storytelling/context beyond retail
- Support conversion without compromising brand integrity
- Balance emotional resonance + commercial performance

## Key Challenges to Solve
- Assortment is intentionally robust with multiple options per routine step + uniform packaging
- Higher cognitive load on customer — need to help them choose confidently
- Help differentiate similar variations (glowy vs. matte moisturizer, clarifying vs. antioxidant treatments)
- Clear education, comparison, use-case framing without overwhelming
- Reduce hesitation from visual similarity across packaging
- Proactively guide decision-making through structure, storytelling, interactive tools (routine flows, comparison cues, quizzes, contextual education)
