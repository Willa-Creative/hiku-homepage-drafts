# Hiku — Brand Identity

## The Brand
- Born from Harper's (age 15) personal experience with breakouts + skin that reacts easily to products
- Gentle, derm-informed skincare designed for acne-prone, sensitive skin
- Skincare that supports who you already are — not who you're told to be
- British-infused, culturally fluent, cool and kind — never clinical, never preachy
- "The coolest thing you can be is yourself"

## Brand Name: h-i-k-u
- **H**eartfelt — warmth, openness, generosity
- **I**ntentional — purpose-driven, conscious skincare
- **K**nowing — derm-informed, high-performance ingredients, global sourcing
- **U**nfiltered — honesty and confidence in a noisy social world

## Brand Personality
- Fresh and current, effortlessly cool, grounded in openness and kindness
- "NYLON at its best" — curiosity over authority, perspective not prescriptions
- Smart tension between polish and vulnerability (Miu Miu energy)
- Joy without twee, commerce without coldness, interaction with purpose

## Proprietary IP
- **hiku kind calmplex™** — proprietary blend for skin that reacts easily, needs consistency over correction

## The Customer
- Skincare-obsessed Gen Z girl, always trying something new
- Influenced by friends and TikTok creators
- Pinches cosmetics from her mum's cabinets
- Been burnt by products that irritated or overpromised
- Not into heavy makeup looks
- Shops Adanola, Skims, RHODE, Summer Fridays. Loves Miu Miu.
- Gets product knowledge peer-to-peer, keen to be up-to-speed on trends
- Likely skews younger with Harper (15) as founder

## The Opportunity
- Gap between clinical authority + playful Gen Z brands
- Hiku sits in the middle: credible, but culturally fluent
- Vast white space for British-infused skincare/lifestyle brand
- "Britishcore" visual language = clear creative brand platform
- NYLON relationship (transatlantic connection true to Harper's experience)

## The Experience (Design North Star)
- Educational, but not preachy
- Visually current — editorial energy inspired, grounded in trust
- A space that feels like a friend with good taste — not a brand talking at you
- Feels like a pause from an industry of rigid labels and prescriptive rules
- Reframes conversation around how skin feels/behaves/changes — not "normal" vs "problematic"
- Reduces self-consciousness; replaces with clarity, confidence, permission

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Summer Friday, Glow Recipe, Rhode, Sincerely Yours, Laneige
**Aspirational (outside category):** Miu Miu, Jacquemus, Crown Affair

## Cultural Values
- Teen mental health in the context of social media
- Community participation ("made with you" — feedback loops, early access, UGC)

## Founder Story (Harper)
After experimenting with online products that didn't suit her skin, Harper ended up with horrible breakouts, extreme sensitivity, and low self-esteem. Only clinical drugstore brands were recommended. She asked: "Why doesn't a brand exist that feels elevated but still gentle enough for my skin?" Collaborated with world-renowned skin experts. "It's what she wishes she'd had from the start."
