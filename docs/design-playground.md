## Daily Beauty Chart / Routine Builder (3 Variations)

### Chart V1 (320:81) — Magazine Editorial
- **Layout:** Large purple circle with "Daily Beauty Chart" script heading
- **Elements:** Face portrait cutout in circle, 4-step product lineup below (Steps 1–4), right column has large serif description text
- **Vibe:** Classic, magazine-editorial
- **Interaction:** Static — displays full routine

### Chart V2 (320:103) — Interactive Mood Wheel
- **Layout:** Pink background, pie-chart-style wheel with face portrait
- **Elements:** "I'm feeling **glowy** today" as mood selector, segmented wheel, product recommendations appear right as stacked cards ("For glowy skin")
- **Vibe:** Interactive quiz-meets-routine-builder
- **Interaction:** Select mood → wheel rotates → products recommended
- **Notable:** Closest to the "Ritual Over Routine" experience principle

### Chart V3 (320:140) — Shoppable Mood Selector
- **Layout:** Cream background, split left/right
- **Left:** "I'm feeling **glowy** today" heading, large circular portrait with arrow navigation (< >)
- **Right:** "For glowy skin" heading, 2x2 product card grid, "Add the Routine" CTA button
- **Vibe:** Most actionable/shoppable of the three
- **Interaction:** Navigate moods with arrows → see corresponding product grid → add full routine to cart
- **Notable:** Best balance of editorial and commerce

## Quiz / Interactive Concepts

### Crystal Ball (367:4) — "Discover your skin mood."
- **Layout:** Centered (1440x1204), cream background
- **Elements:** Large frosted glass crystal ball with swirling pink/mauve mist, question inside: "How do you want your skin to greet the morning?"
- **Instruction:** "Gently rub the crystal ball to awaken the mystical energies within and see what your skin desires. The mist will swirl and reveal your fortune."
- **Vibe:** Playful, magical, mystical
- **Notable:** Directly from experience vision tactics (crystal ball oracle). Most whimsical quiz concept — could be an easter egg or the main quiz entry

### Mirror (370:195) — "What does your skin see?"
- **Layout:** Centered (1344x1196), pink background
- **Elements:** Ornate mirror shape (rounded rectangle with scalloped/archway edges) showing blurred face reflection. Answer options: "Glowy / Calm / Awake"
- **Instruction:** "Every great skincare story begins with a single reflection. Answer the mirror's questions and discover the products meant to find you."
- **Vibe:** Intimate, self-reflective, personal
- **Notable:** Mirror as quiz metaphor — "looking at yourself" to find your routine. More grounded than crystal ball, still playful

## Design Language Emerging

| Element | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Primary palette** | Soft pink, cream/off-white, mauve/dusty purple |
| **Accent colors** | Deep burgundy/brown (text, CTAs), sage green (Matcha product) |
| **Display type** | Elegant serif — used for headings, product names, "Daily Beauty Chart" |
| **Body type** | Clean sans-serif |
| **Photography style** | Extreme close-up skin textures (real, unretouched), macro product application, editorial flatlays, vintage vanity styling |
| **Layout patterns** | Bento grid, circular/organic shapes, editorial collage, split left-right |
| **Shape language** | Circles (charts, portraits), soft rounded rectangles, organic blobs (texture swatches) |
| **Interaction patterns** | Crystal ball rub, mirror reflection, mood wheel rotation, carousel swipe |
| **Content tone** | Sensory ("glowy," "calm," "awake"), curiosity-driven, ritual-focused |

## Key Observations

1. **Real skin is the hero** — Multiple frames lead with extreme close-up unretouched skin, not aspirational beauty shots. Aligns with "unfiltered" brand value.
2. **Routine > Products** — Most commerce modules frame products as part of a routine ("Add the Routine"), not individual purchases. Supports "Ritual Over Routine" principle.
3. **Mood-based navigation** — "I'm feeling glowy/calm/awake today" appears across multiple frames as the primary product discovery mechanism. Replaces traditional skincare concern categories.
4. **British styling cues** — Silver trays, afternoon tea, vanity tables, wax-sealed letters. The townhouse metaphor is being expressed through object styling, not literal architecture.
5. **Two quiz directions** — Crystal ball (playful/magical) vs Mirror (intimate/reflective). Both use blurred imagery that "reveals" based on interaction.
6. **Bento box confirmed** — The homepage layouts use compartmentalized content modules exactly as described in the experience vision.
