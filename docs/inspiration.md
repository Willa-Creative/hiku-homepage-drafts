## Client References (from Discovery Questionnaire)

### 1. Bop Bop World
- **URL:** https://bopbopworld.com
- **Why:** Playfulness done with taste. Easter eggs, interactive moments, joy. Youthful without skewing childish.
- **Visual notes:** Accessories/fashion brand. Interactive "pick your" configurator on homepage. Mix of editorial photography + product grid. Creator spotlight sections. "BopBop, Your Way" UGC community. Clean white base with bold yellow pops. Bold type.
- **Takeaway:** Delight and elevation can coexist

### 2. Summer Fridays
- **URL:** https://summerfridays.com
- **Why:** Shoppable while still feeling like a brand flagship. Commerce + storytelling balanced. Product carousels and UGC feel curated, not noisy.
- **Visual notes:** Soft pastels (pink, blue, cream), dreamy gradients. Hero → community favorites → collection showcase → UGC → category cards → editorial → email capture. Color-blocked sections create rhythm.
- **Takeaway:** Performance-driven ecomm doesn't need to sacrifice brand warmth

### 3. In Common With
- **URL:** https://www.incommonwith.com/collections/all-products
- **Why:** Thoughtful interaction design. PLP toggle for daytime vs. nighttime lighting — small detail that makes experience immersive.
- **Takeaway:** Inspire how skincare textures, finishes, or routines are communicated digitally

### 4. Aesop
- **URL:** https://www.aesop.com
- **Also:** https://www.aesop.com/fragrance.html (product comparison modules)
- **Why:** Smart merchandising for broad assortment with largely uniform packaging (VERY relevant to Hiku's challenge). Clear hierarchy, thoughtful grouping, guided pathways. Comparison modules help understand nuance through approachable language.
- **Visual notes:** Dark/warm tones (deep red, amber, dark green). "New and notable" → "Pleasure in proximity" → "Browse by chapter" → editorial moments → "Recommended reading" quote. Calm, confident, intentional.
- **Takeaway:** Support decision-making through structure, not noise

### 5. Monastery Made
- **URL:** https://monasterymade.com
- **Why:** Good balance between commerce and storytelling. Narrative unfolds naturally alongside products. Guides toward routines by concern. Love their discovery set.
- **Visual notes:** Dark/moody homepage with rich photography. Press logos. Routine-based product grid (Cleanse, Moisturize, Exfoliate). Large editorial imagery with botanical/texture shots. Routine quiz CTA. Subscribe + save.
- **Note from client:** "Definitely too grown up in execution for hiku but I'm personally a fan"

## Design Guardrails

### DO
- Joy, playfulness, interactivity
- Editorial energy, visual currency
- Warmth, trust, approachability
- Smart merchandising, guided pathways
- Immersive product communication
- Sensory copywriting
- Deep but digestible ingredient education

### DON'T
- Twee or childish
- Cold or purely transactional
- Clinical or preachy
- Cookie-cutter / formulaic
- Overwhelming or noisy
- Too "grown up" (Monastery) or too corporate (Laneige)
- Generic DTC template energy
